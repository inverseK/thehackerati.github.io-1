Alex studied marketing as part of Seth Godin's six-month alternative MBA program after graduating from Cornell University’s Interdisciplinary program. He then started an event series called Swagapalooza, and was the first single non-technical founder ever accepted into Y Combinator. This experience made him realize that he should be able to implement his ideas directly, so he taught himself to program as part of his 2012: Year of Code campaign. He’s been working as a software developer with The Hackerati ever since. Alex specializes in frontend web development and has been working on a Big Data analytics platform with one of our largest clients.
